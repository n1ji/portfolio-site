1. Background

At Reddit, I worked on Reticle, an internal AppSec tool for scanning and improving repository hygiene across hundreds of engineering repositories. The goal was to surface ownership, configuration, and security issues early so teams could act quickly.

2. Issues/Problems

Security and compliance checks were inconsistent, leading to blind spots like missing slack channels, unclear ownership, hardcoded secrets, and misconfigured repo files. Teams needed a reliable, automated way to detect and prevent common risks across services.

3. Process/Actions/Decisions

I partnered with mentors to map risk areas, then translated them into actionable Reticle checks guided by Sourcegraph queries and HackerOne patterns. I iterated with engineers on signal quality, balancing strictness with low false negatives and clear remediation guidance.

4. Work

  • Developed and refined Python checks to enforce repository hygiene and surface missing configurations.
  • Wrote pytest suites with mocking to validate scan outputs and prevent regressions.
  • Improved Faceplate frontend elements to make results and next steps more legible.
  • Linked services to their Slack support channels and synced GitHub ownership data for accountability.

5. Result

Reticle now provides consistent, automated enforcement for repo hygiene, with clearer ownership and support-channel mapping. My contributions reduced false negatives, improved visibility for engineers, and strengthened Reddit’s overall security posture.

TL;DR

Built and tested checks in Reticle (Python/pytest), improved UI signals (Faceplate), and integrated Slack/GitHub ownership mapping — enabling consistent repo hygiene and faster remediation across Reddit engineering.
